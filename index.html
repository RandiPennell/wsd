Name origin pika - Japanese for a sound of sparks; a small mouse-like rodent chu - Japanese for a sound of squeaking

Pikachu (Japanese: ピカチュウ Hepburn: Pikachū?) /ˈpiːkəˌtʃuː/ is one of the species of Pokémon creatures from the Pokémon media franchise — a collection of video games, anime, manga, books, trading cards, and other media created by Satoshi Tajiri. Pikachu fight other Pokémon in battles central to the anime, manga, and games of the series.[1] Pikachu is among the most recognizable Pokémon, largely because a Pikachu is a central character in the Pokémon anime series. Pikachu is widely considered the most popular Pokémon,[2] is regarded as the official mascot of the Pokémon franchise, and has become an icon of Japanese culture in recent years.

Pikachu is a mouse Pokemon. He is 1'04" tall and weighs 13.2 pounds. It occasionally uses an electric shock to recharge a fellow Pikachu that is in a weakened state. Pikachu is actually an evolved form of Pichu. However the most evolved form of Pikachu is Raichu. Pikachu is one of the most famous Pokemon characters, his Trainer is Ash.
